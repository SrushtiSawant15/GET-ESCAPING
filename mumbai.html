Mumbai, previously known as Bombay is one of the most populous and biggest cities of Maharashtra. Also the biggest metropolis of this state, Mumbai is also popular as the entertainment and financial capital. It is the largest city of India and fondly called as the city of dreams. It is a place filled with dreamers and people who work hard day and night to achieve their dreams. Mumbai is know for its historical places like Asiatic society, Gateway of India, Kanheri caves, August Kranti Maidan, Mani Bhavan, Elephanta caves, etc. Mumbai locals is the lifeline of Mumbaikars. The city that never sleeps.
It is also known for its various religional places like various churches, mosques and temples. The Basilica of Our Lady of Mount locally kown as Mount Mary church is one of the most ancient church in Mumbai with St. Thomas Cathedral and Afghan church few of them. It is famous for its mosque situated in the middle of the Arabian sea known as Haji Ali. Temples like Mahalaxshmi and Shree Siddhivinayak are few of the temples in Mumbai. As Mumbai is located on the western coast of India, it is also famous for beaches like Juhu beach, Girgoan chowpatthy and Gorai beach. Marines drive also known as Queen’s necklace is one of the major attraction of tourist.
Besides Mumbai is well known for its celebrations of various festivals like Ganesh chaturthi, Shivaji Jayanti, Gudi Padwa, Kala Ghoda Festival, etc. Its cultural diversity is what attracts people which can totally be seen in the culinary richness the city offers. Whether it’s Muslim, Gujarati, South-Indian, Parsi, or Maharashtran, there are a number of dishes to eat in Mumbai with countless flavours, colours, and textures.
